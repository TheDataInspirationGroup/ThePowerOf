# ThePowerOf
An education focussed project to develop core data literacy and AI awareness to students across secondary education. 

# Introducing “The Power Of” Career Resources

## Executive Summary 

The Power Of! is a programme designed to help secondary school students build four essential skills – asking questions, data literacy with AI awareness, storytelling, and presentation. These skills are increasingly essential across all careers, yet many students and educators do not recognise how widely they are used or how they already apply them in everyday life. This project supports educators with simple, ready-to-use resources so they can build confidence first, before delivering these materials to students. Through practical activities and real-world examples, students are encouraged to ask questions, think more deeply, and understand how data is used across different industries. As artificial intelligence becomes more widely used, it is increasingly important that students and educators can question and interpret outputs. Open-source tools such as R programming language provide a powerful and accessible way to explore data, test ideas, and build confidence in understanding whether results are accurate and meaningful. 

The pilot will introduce the first two parts of the programme—focusing on asking questions and understanding data and AI—delivering to educators and students in a minimum of five schools. Educators will take part in an event outside the classroom to explore the resources, build confidence, and share ideas before delivering them to students. The project will also introduce the first step towards a Data Culture Award, recognising schools that embed these skills. Funding will support resource development, pilot delivery, and educator engagement. By building confidence in how to question, interpret, and explore data, this project lays the foundation for a more inclusive and capable future pipeline of data practitioners and future users of open-source tools.

## Problem Definition 

We live in a world where data plays a key role in decision-making across business, healthcare, government, and everyday life. Despite this, data education and awareness of data-related careers in UK secondary schools is very limited. Students are making important decisions about their future without understanding how their skills connect to data-driven careers. Many believe that these careers are only for those strong in maths or coding, which creates unnecessary barriers. 

This gap affects both students and educators. Students interact with data every day—through streaming recommendations, social media, and gaming—yet they do not recognise the skills they are using, such as identifying patterns, making decisions, and asking questions. Educators already use data in their roles but often lack confidence in teaching these skills explicitly or linking them to careers. 

As AI tools become more accessible, there is a growing risk that outputs are accepted without question. Without data and AI literacy, it becomes difficult to sense-check results, identify bias, or understand whether an answer is correct. Building these skills early enables students and educators to move from simply using tools to questioning and understanding them. 

This contributes to a wider skills gap, with [data and digital skill shortages](https://commonslibrary.parliament.uk/research-briefings/cdp-2024-0073/) costing the UK economy an estimated £63 billion each year. Addressing this issue helps students recognise their strengths, build confidence, and understand how their skills apply across many careers, while equipping educators to connect learning to real-world applications. 

Existing [Digdata career challenges](https://digdata.online/library-resource/) have demonstrated strong student engagement but have not yet been embedded into everyday teaching or reached enough educators. The Power Of! builds on this by focusing on educator confidence first, enabling consistent and scalable delivery across schools. 

## Our Proposal 

The Power Of! develops four key skills that are important across all careers: asking questions to solve problems, becoming data and AI aware, communicating ideas through storytelling, and presenting with confidence. The programme is designed so that any educator can deliver it, regardless of subject background, using simple, low-preparation resources. 

The first phase focuses on asking better questions and understanding how data and AI inform decisions. Activities are grounded in familiar contexts such as entertainment, sport, and everyday choices to ensure relevance and engagement. The decision to remove reliance on technology at this stage is intentional, ensuring accessibility across all schools while focusing on foundational thinking skills before introducing tools. 

This approach is already supported in practice. The Aspirations Academies Trust has committed to supporting early development and delivery, inviting the project team to engage with career leaders and senior leadership teams across nine secondary schools at their annual careers conference (July 2026) These teams will then participate in an offsite training day in the Autumn term to explore resources, practise activities, and build confidence in delivering the resources to their students by early 2027. 

To align with industry standards, the Data Culture Award will recognise and celebrate a school’s commitment to embedding meaningful data-driven skills, highlighting how data literacy and AI support student readiness for future careers. The award will encourage whole-school adoption of The Power Of! resources, demonstrating how data can be applied across all subjects. 

## Module Overview – The Power Of! 

The first two modules provide practical, accessible ways to build problem-solving and data literacy skills using visual, non-technical approaches. 

**Module 1** focuses on asking questions to identify the real problem. Educators and students use structured questioning techniques, such as the “5 Whys”, to explore root causes through real world scenarios including business, sport, and environmental challenges. The module also explores how the way questions are asked can influence outcomes. Participants consider how leading questions, assumptions, and bias can shape responses and decisions, helping them recognise the importance of asking clear, neutral, and purposeful questions. This builds a stronger foundation for critical thinking and more accurate problem-solving. 

**Module 2** builds on this by introducing data literacy with AI awareness. Participants explore simple datasets, identify patterns, and consider how data informs decisions. They also reflect on how AI uses data and where bias may exist. By linking back to Module 1, learners understand how biased questions or assumptions can influence the data collected and, in turn, the outputs generated by AI systems. 

This module also introduces a pathway to further exploration using open-source tools. Educators and students can extend their learning using real-world datasets such as TidyTuesday, asking questions and exploring patterns in greater depth. Tools such as R programming language provide a powerful and accessible way to work with data, helping learners move from understanding concepts to actively exploring and validating insights. 

This progression creates a clear pathway from early data awareness to future engagement with open-source tools, supporting a more diverse pipeline of confident data users, with guided activities introducing tools such as R programming language through relatable contexts to build confidence and reduce technology anxiety. 

**Module 1: Asking Questions** 
Educator Pathway - Scenarios, problem framing, visual mapping, structured questioning 
Students Pathway - Hands-on activities using real-world scenarios such as shops, sports, and environmental issues 
Techniques / Tools - 5 Whys, problem trees, cause-and-effect diagrams 

**Module 2: Data Literacy with AI Awareness** 
Educator Pathway - Working with simple datasets, identifying patterns, reflecting on AI use 
Students Pathway - Visual data activities, scenario mapping, trend exploration 
Techniques / Tools - Data interpretation, trend analysis, root cause mapping, AI awareness prompts 

These modules are fully inclusive and do not require specialist technology, making them easy to deliver in any classroom. They build educator confidence first, ensuring consistent delivery and long-term impact. 
 
## Project Plan and Timeline 

**Discovery and Planning - June-July 2026**
Activity: Market research will be conducted in advance of the annual careers conference in July with the Aspirations Academies Trust, gathering insights from career leaders and their SLT across nine secondary schools. Surveys will explore current knowledge of data and AI-related careers, barriers to delivering these skills personally and within school settings, where educators source materials from and why, and how they are currently 
preparing students for a data-driven world. The Power Of! will be introduced at the conference, alongside discussion of the proposed training day and pilot delivery. 

**Development and Design - July-September 2026**
Activity - Development of educator and student resources for Module 1 (Asking Questions) and Module 2 (Data Literacy with AI Awareness). Training day planning will take place, including agenda design and interactive 
workshop structure. 

**Testing and Final Preparation - October 2026**
Activity: Resources will be reviewed and tested with one school within the trust to include classroom testing with students to ensure clarity and usability. Final adjustments will be made based on feedback. Visual 
aids and discussion materials will be professionally printed in preparation for delivery. 

**Delivery and Implementation - November 2026**
Activity: Official launch of The Power Of! resources through an in-person training day. A minimum of 5 schools within the trust will be selected to pilot student delivery. Additional online training will be offered to support educators, alongside guided delivery within schools to ensure confidence and consistency. 

**Evaluation and Reflection - January 2027**
Activity: Post-delivery focus groups will be conducted with participating schools to gather feedback on delivery, impact, and areas for improvement. Insights will inform future development and scaling. 

**Final Report - February 2027**
A final report will be submitted, outlining delivery, key learnings, and impact to the ISC grant board. We will award our first Data Culture Awards.

## Success 

The project will be successful when the first two modules have been delivered in five schools and educators are confidently using the resources with students. Success will be measured through educator and student feedback, engagement levels, and evidence of increased confidence in delivering and applying these skills, including repeated use of resources, increased student participation, and clear progression from questioning to data interpretation. Over time, success will also be reflected in increased interest from educators and students in exploring data further, including through open-source tools such as R programming language. 

The Data Culture Award will play a key role in this, providing schools with a framework to recognise and demonstrate their commitment to building data literacy with AI, while enabling scalable impact across multi-academy trusts nationwide. 

Future phases will expand the programme to include storytelling and presentation skills, introduce optional use of open-source tools such as R programming language, and explore opportunities to extend the approach into primary education, enabling earlier engagement and long-term impact.
